Process

How I Work with AI

Producing good AI-generated images requires intentional prompting — not just typing a description and hoping. Here's the workflow behind the work above.

Step 01
Define the brief
Before generating anything, I establish what the image needs to do — what mood, what context, who sees it, and what it sits alongside. For Seekive this meant understanding each activity category deeply before generating assets for it.
ResearchPlanning
Step 02
Craft the prompt
Prompting is a technical skill. I build prompts with precise subject description, style direction, lighting, camera angle, colour palette, mood, and negative prompts to exclude unwanted elements. Iteration is built in from the start.
Prompt EngineeringIteration
Step 03
Generate & refine
Multiple generations are produced and evaluated against the brief. Prompt parameters are adjusted based on what's working — subject framing, composition, style consistency, and cohesion across a set of images.
GenerationQuality Control
Step 04
Post-process & apply
Selected outputs are refined in Adobe Photoshop or Lightroom where needed — colour matching, cropping, and any compositing. Final assets are prepared for their target format, whether app UI, web, or print.
PhotoshopLightroomCompositing
Wider AI Use

AI Across Every Discipline

Generative image work is one part of how I use AI. Across all my work — development, video, marketing, writing — AI through prompting is a core part of the process.

🤖
AI as a core tool, not an afterthought
I use AI to research topics in depth, plan project structure and timelines, draft scripts, copy, and documentation, generate visual and creative assets, and refine outputs iteratively. On Seekive, AI is embedded at the product level — real-time recommendations via Google Gemini, and AI-generated imagery used throughout the app's UI. On video projects, AI assists with research, scripting and shot planning before I ever open Premiere Pro. This breadth is reflected in my Google Cloud certifications in Generative AI and Image Generation, completed in 2024.
